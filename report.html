The following set up is used to execute all the experiments.
  1. Operating system :Linux mint
  2. R version: 4.0.2
  3. R studio: Version 1.3.1093
All the reports mentioned in APA style at the end of each test. For reference, some of the reports have been reported here from prepare, model and explore page.
  1. Reporting normality : Second period grade was assessed for normality. Visual inspection of the histogram and QQ-Plot did not exhibhit any issues with skewness and kurtosis. The standardized score for skewness(1.91) and the standardized score for kurtosis (-0.70) was can be considered acceptable using the criteria proposed by West, Finch and Curran (1996). 100% of standardized scores for grade 2 fall within the bounds +/- 3.29, using the guidance of Field, Miles and Field (2013) the data can be considered to be a normal distribution (m=12.23, sd=2.47, n=382).
  2. Reporting correlation: The relationship between age and family support was investigated using a Kendall correlation. A weak positive correlation was found (tau =0.11, n=380, p=0.02).
  3. Reporting difference test with Cohen's d effect: An independent-samples t-test was conducted to compare grade obtained in test 2 for students who have family support and those who do not have family support. No significant difference in the scores for grade obtained was found (M=12.06, SD= 2.61 for respondents who do not have family support, M= 12.34, SD= 2.38 for students who have family support), (t(382)= -1.05, p = 0.29). Cohen's d also indicated a very small effect size (-0.11).
  4. Reporting difference test with eta squared effect: An independent-samples t-test was conducted to compare grade obtained in test 2 for students who have family support and those who do not have family support. No significant difference in the scores for grade obtained was found (M=12.06, SD= 2.61 for respondents who do not have family support, M= 12.34, SD= 2.38 for students who have family support), (t(382)= -1.05, p = 0.29). A very small effect size was also indicated by the eta squared value (0.003).
  5. Reporting differences for Mann-whitney test: A Mann-Whitney test indicated that the final grade was significantly greater for students who have taken extra paid classes(Mdn = 12, IQR = 2.75) than for students who have not paid extra for classes(Mdn = 13, IQR = 3), (U = 5910.5, z = 2.38, p = .017, r = 0.12)
  6. Reporting linear regression: A simple linear regression was carried out to investigate whether total absences could significantly predict student's second period grade. A significant regression equation was found (F(1,380)=15.89 , p < .05 ), with an R2 of 0.04. Participants second period grade is equal to 12.61 - 0.1 (abesences), where absences represents number of days student missed the school. Absences is a significant predictor of second period grade.
